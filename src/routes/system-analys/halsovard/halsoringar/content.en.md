# Community health rings

[← Back to the main article](..)

**Summary**: Networks of peer support groups (health rings) focused on specific health challenges: mental illness, type 2 diabetes reversal, loneliness among the elderly, postpartum support, and chronic pain. The rings are led by trained laypeople (circle leaders) and are open to anyone who wants to participate. Participation can be financed via redirected wellness allowances or social prescribing from a primary care center. The goal is to reclaim the social domain of health – to create connection, meaning, and practical support outside the medical sphere. The model is built on the Swedish tradition of civil society associations and international evidence for the effectiveness of peer support.

## The mechanism

Today, the primary care center is often the only institution offering support for mild mental illness, lifestyle-related diseases, or social isolation. The result is the medicalization of everyday problems and an overburdened primary care system. At the same time, there is a strong Swedish tradition of study associations, the temperance movement, and patient organizations – but these are rarely integrated with the healthcare system.

**The measure**: A **community health ring** is a regular gathering (physical or digital) where:

  - A trained circle leader (often with personal experience of the condition) facilitates conversations, knowledge sharing, and practical activities.
  - Participants support each other in managing their situation – whether it involves reducing stress, eating better, exercising, or breaking isolation.
  - The rings are **non-clinical** and do not replace medical treatment, but function as a powerful complement.
  - Financing occurs through **existing wellness allowances** (via employers or the region) which can be used for membership in certified rings. This requires no new budget funds, only a redefinition of what counts as wellness.

**The evidence base**:

  - Peer support reduces doctor visits by 20–30% for participants (Cochrane Review, 2020).
  - Diabetes reversal in a group format (e.g., in the UK) has proven more effective than individual counseling.
  - Social prescribing in the UK has reduced the burden on primary care by an average of 28%.

**Examples of health rings**:

| Type | Target group | Activities | Expected effect |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------------|
| The Diabetes Ring | People with type 2 diabetes or prediabetes | Dietary review (low-carb diet), walks, recipe sharing | Reduced medication, weight loss, fewer complications |
| The Elderly Ring | Seniors experiencing loneliness | Fika (coffee breaks), walks, reading aloud, tech help | Reduced depression, fewer fall accidents (via increased mobility) |
| The Parenting Ring | New parents | Open house, stroller walks, sleep coaching | Reduced postpartum depression, increased self-confidence |
| The Recovery Ring | People with mild mental illness | Discussion group, mindfulness, sleep hygiene | Reduced anxiety, fewer doctor visits |
| The Pain Ring | People with chronic pain | Movement exercises, relaxation, peer support | Better pain management, reduced opioid need |

## Pilot design – 3 municipalities first

**Pilot phase 1 (years 1‑3)**: Three municipalities test the model with health rings within at least two categories each. Financing is achieved through the reallocation of existing wellness funds and potentially supplementary project grants.

| Municipality | Type | Health rings | Number of participants (goal) | Partners |
|--------|-----|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Rinkeby (Stockholm)** | Suburb with high ill-health rates | The Diabetes Ring, The Parenting Ring | 100 | Studieförbundet Vuxenskolan, Rinkeby Folkets Hus, local primary care centers |
| **Nynäshamn** | Smaller town with a mixed population | The Elderly Ring, The Recovery Ring | 80 | SPF Seniorerna, Studieförbundet Bilda, the municipality's elderly care |
| **Östersund** | Regional city with rural surroundings | The Pain Ring, The Diabetes Ring | 120 | Studieförbundet ABF, Jämtland County Council, local gyms |

**Organizational model**:

  - **Circle leader training**: Study associations (e.g., ABF, Sensus, Vuxenskolan) offer a short training course (2–3 days) for prospective circle leaders. The training covers group dynamics, basic health information (not medical advice), and administrative routines.
  - **Certification**: Rings that meet quality criteria (trained leader, regular meetings, participant influence) are certified by a national council (see below) and become eligible for wellness allowances.
  - **Local anchoring**: Each ring collaborates with a local primary care center that can "prescribe" the ring (social prescribing) to patients deemed to benefit from peer support.
  - **Follow-up**: Participants fill out a well-being survey (e.g., WHO-5) at the start and after 6 months. Data is aggregated anonymously for evaluation.

**Evaluation**:

  - Number of participants per ring and municipality (goal: at least 20 participants per ring after 6 months).
  - Change in self-rated health (WHO-5).
  - Change in the number of primary care center visits for participants compared to a control group.
  - Participants' experience of coherence and social support.
  - Cost per participant compared to the equivalent healthcare intervention.

## Budget and financing

| Item | Amount (per municipality, 3 years) | Total 3 municipalities |
|------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Training of circle leaders (10 leaders × SEK 3,000) | SEK 30,000 | SEK 90,000 |
| Venue rental and materials (the rings use existing venues like libraries, parish halls, etc.) | SEK 20,000/year | SEK 180,000 |
| Marketing and recruitment | SEK 15,000 | SEK 45,000 |
| Evaluation and research support | SEK 50,000 (one-time) | SEK 150,000 |
| Compensation for circle leaders (fee, SEK 200/hr, 2 hrs/week, 40 weeks/year) | SEK 16,000/year per leader | SEK 960,000 (for an average of 5 leaders per municipality) |
| **Total per municipality** | **approx. SEK 280,000** (over 3 years) | **approx. SEK 1.4 million** |

**Financing**:

  - **Wellness allowance**: An employee can currently receive up to SEK 5,000/year in wellness allowance. By certifying health rings as approved wellness activities, participants can pay a small fee themselves (e.g., SEK 500/semester) using their allowance.
  - **Municipal public health funds**: Many municipalities already have a budget for public health interventions.
  - **Project grants**: The Public Health Agency of Sweden or the Swedish Inheritance Fund (Allmänna arvsfonden) can finance pilot projects.

**Note**: This is a **fractional investment** compared to the cost of a single unnecessary doctor's visit (approx. SEK 2,000) or sick leave (approx. SEK 100,000 per year).

## Regulatory changes required

1.  **Amendment to the Swedish Tax Agency's rules for wellness allowances** – Clarify that participation in certified health rings counts as a wellness activity. This only requires a clarification from the Tax Agency, not a legislative change.

2.  **The Social Services Act (SoL)** – Clarify that municipalities are allowed to use social services funds for preventive interventions in the form of health rings, especially for the elderly and people with mental illness.

3.  **The Health and Medical Services Act (HSL)** – Task the regions with developing routines for social prescribing, where healthcare professionals can refer patients to certified health rings.

4.  **The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** – Develop a simple consent template so the rings can collect aggregated data for evaluation without violating privacy legislation.

## Expected resistance and answers

### "This is not evidence-based – we shouldn't waste tax money on hobby activities."

**Answer**: On the contrary. Social prescribing and peer support have a robust evidence base (see Cochrane Review, NHS England). To **not** use these methods is to waste tax money on more expensive and less effective interventions.

### "Who takes responsibility if something goes wrong? What if a participant gets sick during a walk?"

**Answer**: The rings are not a medical operation. Participants are responsible for their own health. Circle leaders are trained in basic first aid and routines for contacting 112 in emergency situations. The same division of responsibility applies as for any walking group.

### "This is only suitable for already healthy and resourceful people."

**Answer**: The pilot includes Rinkeby and other areas with lower socioeconomic status. The rings are free of charge or have a low fee (which can be paid with the wellness allowance). Circle leaders are recruited locally and often have personal experience of the target group's challenges.

### "The primary care centers won't collaborate – they already have too much to do."

**Answer**: Social prescribing can initially be a simple referral ("here is a folder about the diabetes ring in your area"). When healthcare professionals see that patients feel better and return less frequently, the incentive to continue becomes strong.

### "Are there any Swedish examples?"

**Answer**: Yes, but not in a cohesive way. **Fountain Houses** (for people with mental illness) are built on peer support. **The Swedish Diabetes Association** has local groups. **SPF Seniorerna** and **PRO** (pensioner organizations) have walking groups. What is missing is systematic integration with the healthcare system and financing via wellness allowances.

## Swedish examples to build upon

  - **The Fountain House in Stockholm, Gothenburg, Malmö** – Member-driven activities for people with mental illness. Evidence of reduced inpatient care episodes.
  - **Diabetes Stockholm** – Local associations with member meetings, lectures, and walks.
  - **SPF Seniorerna's walking groups** – Over 10,000 participants nationwide.
  - **The Swedish Heart and Lung Association (Riksförbundet HjärtLung)** – Peer support after a heart attack or COPD diagnosis.

## What you can do tomorrow

**If you are a municipal politician**: Initiate a pilot project with the study associations. Appoint a contact person for social prescribing. Allocate SEK 100,000 to train the first circle leaders.

**If you work at a primary care center**: Start by mapping out which local associations and groups already exist. Create a simple list that staff can hand out to patients.

**If you are a private citizen**: Join an existing association that works with health. Ask your study association if they can start a health ring in your area of interest. Sign up as a circle leader.
