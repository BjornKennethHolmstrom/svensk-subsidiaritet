# Health capital accounts

[← Back to the main article](..)

**Summary**: A personal, annual pot (e.g., SEK 3,000–5,000) that every resident can use for approved preventive health interventions: dietary counseling, smoking cessation, stress management, membership in health rings, or exercise activities. Unused funds are saved for the next year, creating a compound interest effect for long-term health investments. The model shifts resources from reactive treatment to proactive prevention without requiring new tax money – it is financed through the reallocation of existing healthcare budgets and reduced costs over time. The goal: to give individuals both financial incentives and practical opportunities to invest in their future health.

## The mechanism

Today's system is built to pay for illness, not for health. The high-cost threshold (högkostnadsskyddet) subsidizes doctor visits and medications, while those who want to take a stress management course or buy vegetables have to pay out of their own pocket. This is a structural distortion that penalizes those who try to stay healthy.

**The measure**: A **health capital account** is an individual account that:

  - Is allocated to every resident annually (e.g., SEK 3,000 for adults, SEK 5,000 for children and the elderly over 75).
  - Can be used for a wide range of preventive services and products scientifically proven to improve health.
  - Unused funds accumulate over time and can be used later in life – e.g., for rehabilitation, home adaptation, or reinforced home care in old age.
  - Is administered by a national authority (e.g., the Swedish eHealth Agency) via a digital platform linked to BankID.

**Examples of approved expenses**:

| Category | Example | Evidence for health effect |
|----------|---------|-------------------------|
| Physical activity | Gym membership, swimming pool, dance class, personal trainer | Reduced risk of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, depression |
| Diet and nutrition | Dietary counseling (registered dietitian), cooking classes, subscription to recipe service | Weight loss, improved metabolic health |
| Mental health | Mindfulness course, CBT-based self-help, stress management programs | Reduced anxiety and depression |
| Community and social health | Membership in health rings, association fees | Reduced loneliness, increased well-being |
| Prevention of chronic disease | Smoking cessation, diabetes prevention programs, fall prevention for the elderly | Avoided future healthcare costs |

**Financing model**:

The accounts are financed through a combination of:

1.  **Reprioritization of existing healthcare budgets**: A share (e.g., 5%) of the regions' budget for outpatient care is earmarked for health capital accounts instead of going to reactive treatment.
2.  **Reduced costs in the long term**: As the population gets healthier, the pressure on acute and specialist care decreases. The savings are reinvested in the system.
3.  **Potential co-financing from employers**: Just like with the wellness allowance (friskvårdsbidrag), employers can top up the account.

**Difference from the wellness allowance**:

| Aspect | Wellness allowance | Health capital account |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Target group | Only employees | All residents |
| Administrator | Employer | State authority |
| Accumulation | No, "use it or lose it" | Yes, saved over time |
| Area of use | Narrow (exercise) | Broad (physical, mental, social health) |

## Pilot design – 3 municipalities or regions

**Pilot phase 1 (years 1‑4)**: Three geographical areas with different population compositions test the model with a starting capital of SEK 3,000 per resident over 16 years of age.

| Area | Residents (16+) | Budget (SEK 3,000/person) | Special focus |
|--------|---------------|-------------------------|----------------|
| **Region Gotland** | \~50,000 | SEK 150 million | Holistic approach: island structure facilitates evaluation and control |
| **Angered district, Gothenburg** | \~40,000 | SEK 120 million | Socio-economically vulnerable area, high ill health |
| **Pajala municipality** | \~5,000 | SEK 15 million | Sparsely populated area, elderly population, long distances to healthcare |

**Technical solution**:

  - **Digital platform**: An app and web portal (integrated with 1177.se) where the user can view their balance, search among approved providers, and register purchases.
  - **Approval process**: A national council (with representation from the Public Health Agency, the National Board of Health and Welfare, and patient associations) approves which services and products are covered. The list is updated annually based on evidence.
  - **Payment**: The user pays with a regular debit card and uploads a receipt in the app, or the provider bills the account directly (via a virtual card linked to the account).
  - **Control**: Spot checks and automatic flagging of suspicious transactions.

**Evaluation**:

  - Utilization rate of the account (proportion of the population using the funds, and within which categories).
  - Change in self-rated health (EQ-5D or similar) after 2 and 4 years.
  - Change in healthcare consumption (primary care visits, specialist visits, inpatient care) compared to matched control areas.
  - Accumulated savings in the accounts (as an indicator of future prevention potential).
  - Cost analysis: total costs for the accounts minus reduced healthcare costs.

## Budget and financing (pilot phase)

| Item | Gotland | Angered | Pajala | Total |
|------|---------|---------|--------|--------|
| Capital for residents' accounts (SEK 3,000 × res) | 150 MSEK | 120 MSEK | 15 MSEK | 285 MSEK |
| Platform development (one-time, shared by pilots) | 10 MSEK | 10 MSEK | 5 MSEK | 25 MSEK |
| Platform operation (per year) | 2 MSEK | 2 MSEK | 1 MSEK | 5 MSEK/year |
| Administration and support (per year) | 3 MSEK | 3 MSEK | 1 MSEK | 7 MSEK/year |
| Evaluation and research | 5 MSEK (one-time) | 5 MSEK | 3 MSEK | 13 MSEK |
| **Total over 3 years** | \~180 MSEK | \~150 MSEK | \~30 MSEK | **\~360 MSEK** |

**Financing sources**:

  - **Reallocation of regional healthcare budgets**: The pilot regions allocate a portion of their outpatient care budget to the accounts. Since the accounts are expected to reduce outpatient visits, this is a cost-neutral transition in the long term.
  - **State stimulus funds**: The government can provide funds for platform development and evaluation via innovation agencies (Vinnova, Forte).
  - **EU funds**: Horizon Europe has calls for innovative health financing models.

**Note**: SEK 360 million over three years corresponds to **0.1% of Sweden's annual healthcare costs**. It is a very small investment to test a model that can slow down the unsustainable cost increase.

## Regulatory changes required

1.  **Act on Health Capital Accounts**: A new law defining the account's purpose, administration, supervision, and rights. The law should also stipulate that unused funds are personal and cannot be garnished for debts.

2.  **Amendment to the Social Insurance Code**: Clarify that withdrawals from the health capital account do not affect the right to sickness benefit, income support (social assistance), or other benefits.

3.  **Tax legislation**: The health capital account should be tax-free both upon allocation and withdrawal (similar to today's wellness allowance). Any interest on accumulated capital can be taxed at a low rate.

4.  **The Patient Data Act and GDPR**: The account handles sensitive personal data (purchases of health services). Clear regulation of data protection and consent is required.

5.  **Procurement rules**: Since the account can be used with many different providers, a simplified procurement procedure or an approval system is needed instead of traditional LOU (Public Procurement Act) procurement.

## Expected resistance and answers

### "This is throwing money at the already healthy – the sick get nothing."

**Answer**: Everyone gets the same amount, but those with the greatest health needs can use the money more strategically (e.g., for smoking cessation or diabetes prevention). Additionally, healthcare is relieved, so the sick experience shorter queues. It is a universal tool that benefits everyone.

### "People will cheat and buy candy with the money."

**Answer**: Only approved providers and products are covered. A digital control system with spot checks makes cheating difficult. Experiences from similar systems (e.g., in Singapore) show that fraud is marginal.

### "This will be expensive – where will the money come from?"

**Answer**: They are taken from an already unsustainably growing healthcare budget. The question is not *if* we should invest in prevention, but *when*. Every krona not invested in prevention today costs seven kronor in future healthcare.

### "It's unfair that those who are already healthy get to save money for the future."

**Answer**: That is exactly the point – to reward and encourage a healthy life. Furthermore, even people with chronic illnesses can use the money to improve their quality of life and slow the disease progression.

### "Are there any international examples?"

**Answer**: Yes. **Singapore** has had Medisave since 1984 – mandatory health savings accounts that cover healthcare costs. The **USA** has Health Savings Accounts (HSA) with tax advantages. **Germany** is testing personal health budgets in pilot projects. Sweden would be the first in the Nordic region with a universal model.

## Swedish examples to build upon

  - **The wellness allowance (Friskvårdsbidraget)**: A proven system for subsidizing exercise and physical activity. The health capital account is a broader and more inclusive version.
  - **Culture vouchers (Kulturscheckar)**: Some municipalities give the elderly vouchers for cultural activities – a simpler form of earmarked accounts.
  - **Activity cards for children and youth**: Available in several municipalities, giving children the opportunity to try out sports and leisure activities.
  - **The Swedish eHealth Agency's infrastructure**: Already established to handle digital prescriptions and health data – can be expanded for health capital accounts.

## What you can do tomorrow

**If you are a regional politician**: Submit a motion for a feasibility study for health capital accounts in your region. Ask the regional director to investigate financing opportunities and administrative prerequisites.

**If you are a Member of Parliament**: Submit a motion to appoint a government inquiry on "A national system for health capital accounts." Use this text as a basis.

**If you are an official at the Public Health Agency or the National Board of Health and Welfare**: Initiate a knowledge base on the evidence for personal health budgets. Monitor international examples and network with authorities in other countries.

**If you are a private individual**: Start keeping a "health ledger" of your preventive investments – what do you spend on gyms, supplements, massages? Think about how much you would save if you received a dedicated account for this.
