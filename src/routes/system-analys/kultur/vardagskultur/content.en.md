# Grants for everyday culture

[← Back to the main article](..)

**Summary**: SEK 5,000 to non-professional cultural creators—Somali grandmothers recording cooking songs, teenagers documenting skateboard culture, pensioners writing local history in their own language. Simple video application, no qualifications required. 10,000 grants per year = SEK 50 million, less than 0.5% of the Arts Council's budget. The purpose: to recognize that most cultural creation takes place outside institutions.

## The mechanism

Today, almost all cultural support goes to professional actors with a documented track record, education, and often membership in industry organizations. This excludes the vast majority of actual cultural creators—those who create at home, in associations, online, in the neighborhood.

**The measure**: A new grant of SEK 5,000 per project, with no requirements for:

  - Previous exhibitions or publications
  - Artistic education
  - Membership in any organization
  - Translation into Swedish

**What the grant can be used for**:

  - Recording equipment (microphone, camera)
  - Materials (fabric, paint, clay)
  - Renting a venue for an evening
  - Printing a small edition of books or fanzines
  - Time to complete the work

**What the grant may not be used for**:

  - Ongoing operating costs
  - Investments in a professional studio that is then rented out
  - Travel outside Sweden

## The application process – designed for simplicity

**Step 1**: Record a short video (max 3 minutes) where you show or talk about what you want to create. State what you need the money for. The video is uploaded to a simple platform (MinKultur.se).

**Step 2**: No form with a personal identity number, certificates, or references. Just a button: "Submit". The system generates a digital receipt.

**Step 3**: Assessment is done by **previous recipients**—not by professional cultural bureaucrats. Every person who received a grant one year can participate in assessing other people's applications the next year (voluntarily, for a small fee). This creates a self-reinforcing loop of review and learning.

**Step 4**: Notification within 30 days. The money is paid directly to a Swish account linked to the personal identity number (to reduce the risk of fraud).

## Pilot design – ten municipalities first

**Pilot phase 1 (year 1)**: Ten municipalities with different profiles (large city, rural area, high proportion of foreign-born) receive SEK 1 million each—a total of SEK 10 million. Each municipality distributes 200 grants of SEK 5,000.

**Selection criteria for pilot municipalities**:

  - Malmö (high proportion of foreign-born, strong association culture)
  - Kiruna (sparsely populated area, Sami and newly arrived culture)
  - Botkyrka (great diversity, existing cultural life outside the establishment)
  - Umeå (student city, youth subcultures)
  - Gothenburg (immigrant music scene)
  - Östersund (local heritage culture, small-scale authorship)
  - Norrköping (industrial heritage, working-class culture)
  - Järfälla (suburb of Stockholm, low degree of professional cultural life)
  - Hässleholm (rural municipality with smallholder tradition)
  - Stockholm (to test whether even central Stockholm has invisible everyday culture)

**Evaluation**: After one year, the following are measured:

  - Number of submitted applications (target: at least 500 per municipality)
  - Proportion who have never previously received cultural support (target: \>80%)
  - Proportion with a foreign background or mother tongue other than Swedish (target: proportional to the municipality's population)
  - Perceived effect – survey to recipients ("Did you feel seen as a cultural creator?")

## Budget and financing

| Item | Annual cost |
|------|--------------|
| 10,000 grants of SEK 5,000 | SEK 50 million |
| Platform (MinKultur.se) operation and development | SEK 2 million |
| Assessor fees (1000 people at SEK 2000) | SEK 2 million |
| Municipal administration (0.5 full-time per pilot municipality) | SEK 3 million |
| Evaluation and dissemination | SEK 1 million |
| **Total** | **SEK 58 million** |

**Financing**: Reallocation within the Arts Council's budget (total approx. SEK 12 billion annually). SEK 58 million is **0.48%**—a minor reprioritization from large institutions to everyday culture.

## Regulatory changes required

1.  **New regulation at the Arts Council** – "Grants for non-professional cultural creation". Proposed text: *"Support may be provided to natural persons to carry out cultural expressions in the form of, for example, music, storytelling, crafts, film, poetry, dance, or other artistic design. The applicant does not need to be a professional or have previous qualifications."*
2.  **Change in the Freedom of the Press Act** (regarding grants for printed matter)? No, not necessary—the grant is general, not linked to a publishing permit.
3.  **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** – The platform must handle personal data (videos, Swish linked to personal identity numbers). But since it is public administration, this can be solved with standard solutions.

## Expected resistance and answers

### "This will be a wild explosion of low-quality junk."

**Answer**: Quality is not a universal characteristic. What one immigrant group experiences as beautiful and meaningful may not fit into the aesthetics of a Stockholm curator. The purpose is not to produce "good" culture according to professional standards—but to recognize that culture is what people do. Let 10,000 flowers bloom. Some will be ugly flowers in the eyes of others. That is the price of democracy.

### "Professional cultural creators will be disadvantaged."

**Answer**: Professionals already have hundreds of forms of support: working grants, project grants, exhibition compensation, purchases for museums, tour support, etchttps://www.google.com/search?q=.. The everyday culture grant is a small pot (0.5% of the Arts Council's budget) that does not compete with professional funding. It is an addition, not a reallocation from professionals.

### "Who protects against fraud and duplicate applications?"

**Answer**: The platform links applications to personal identity numbers. A person can receive a maximum of one grant per year. Swish payout to a verified account. Spot checks (5% of grants) where the recipient must show a receipt for what the money was used for. If someone cheats (e.g., buys a video game instead of recording equipment)—repayment demand and suspension for two years.

### "Assessment by previous recipients – they have no competence."

**Answer**: What competence is required to determine whether a video of a Somali grandmother singing a cooking song is "worth" SEK 5,000? The point is that there is no objective quality assessment—just a check that the application is genuine and feasible. Previous recipients can easily see if an application is frivolous or fabricated. That is enough.

### "The municipalities will shut it down – no energy."

**Answer**: That is why the pilot is first with ten municipalities that *want* to test it. After a successful pilot, a standardized model is created that other municipalities can copy with minimal administration. The platform handles the heavy lifting—the municipality only needs to appoint a contact person for information and simple handling of appeals.

## Swedish examples to build on

  - **Kulturbryggan** (The Swedish Arts Council) – already testing grassroots financing, but retaining requirements for professional anchoring. The everyday culture grant goes further.
  - **The Swedish Inheritance Fund (Allmänna arvsfonden)** – has "small change" for children's and young people's ideas, but is not open to adults.
  - **Braille for the visually impaired** – a model for how grants can be simple and uncomplicated.
  - **Village association grants (Byalagsbidrag)** (The Swedish Board of Agriculture) – available for rural development, but requires an association. The everyday culture grant goes to individuals.

No Swedish model does exactly this today—so we are creating a new one.

## What you can do tomorrow

**If you are a municipal politician**: Submit a budget request of SEK 1 million for "grants for non-professional cultural creation" in your municipality. Use the text above as a basis.

**If you are a culture secretary**: Start a simple test version with post-it notes and a petty cash box—let 10 people in your area apply for SEK 5,000 each. Learn from the process.

**If you are a cultural creator (professional or not)**: Spread the idea to your networks. The more people who demand that "everyday culture counts," the faster the change will happen.
