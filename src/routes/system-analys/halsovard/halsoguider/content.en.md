# Health guides via popular education

[← Back to the main article](..)

**Summary**: Certification paths through study associations and folk high schools for health guides – locally rooted coaches who work preventively with lifestyle, self-care, and social health. The health guide is not a medical profession but a complementary resource that relieves primary care centers and meets people in their everyday lives. The training is short (a few weeks), practically oriented, and builds on the Swedish popular education tradition. The goal: to create a new professional category that can handle the 80% of health determinants located outside the clinic walls – at a tenth of the cost of a doctor's visit.

## The mechanism

Today, there is a lack of an intermediate level between self-care and professional healthcare. Those struggling with stress, sleep problems, dietary changes, or loneliness are directed either to Dr. Google or to an overburdened primary care center. The result is that doctors and nurses spend valuable time on advice that could just as easily be given by a knowledgeable layperson, while patients with complex needs have to wait.

**The measure**: A **health guide** is a person with:

  - A shorter training (2–4 weeks) via a study association or folk high school, focusing on:
      - Basic health information (diet, movement, sleep, stress).
      - Motivational interviewing (MI) and a coaching approach.
      - Knowledge of local resources (associations, green areas, activities).
      - Ethics, integrity, and boundaries regarding medical advice.
  - Employment in a municipality, housing company, library, or non-profit organization.
  - A mandate to be available for individual conversations, group activities, and guiding within the healthcare system.

**Difference from existing professions**:

| Professional role | Education | Focus | Cost per hour |
|-----------|------------|-------|-------------------|
| Doctor (primary care) | 5.5 years + internship | Diagnosis, treatment, prescribing | \~SEK 1,200 |
| Nurse | 3 years | Nursing, sampling, advice | \~SEK 700 |
| Health coach (private) | Varying, often expensive certifications | Individual coaching, often diet/movement | \~SEK 800–1,500 |
| **Health guide (popular education)** | **2–4 weeks** | **Lifestyle support, community, guiding** | **\~SEK 150–200** |

**Examples of tasks**:

  - Staffs a "health desk" at the library two afternoons a week.
  - Leads walking groups for seniors.
  - Helps a newly diagnosed diabetic interpret dietary advice and find recipes.
  - Supports a person with mild anxiety in finding a suitable health ring.
  - Visits residential areas to inform about wellness and local activities.

## Pilot design – 3 municipalities with different profiles

**Pilot phase 1 (years 1‑3)**: Three municipalities each hire a cohort of health guides (4–6 people) placed in different types of operations.

| Municipality | Placement | Number of guides | Target group | Partners |
|--------|-----------|--------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Borås** | Library, citizen center | 6 | Broad public, focusing on newly arrived immigrants and the elderly | Studieförbundet Vuxenskolan, University of Borås (evaluation) |
| **Umeå** | Housing company Bostaden, primary care center | 4 | Tenants in vulnerable areas, students | Umeå University (education), Region Västerbotten |
| **Gotland** | Rural development, community centers | 4 | Elderly in rural areas, farmers | Hela Gotlands folkhögskola, Region Gotland |

**Structure of the training**:

  - **Theory (1 week)**: Basic health information, motivational interviewing, ethics, and law. Conducted at a folk high school or study association.
  - **Practicum (1 week)**: Job shadowing at a primary care center, nursing home, and within social services.
  - **Specialization (elective, 1 week)**: Elderly health, children and youth, integration, or mental health.
  - **Examination**: Simple knowledge test and practical case.

**Forms of employment**:

  - Municipal employment (e.g., within leisure or public health administration).
  - Employment via a municipal housing company.
  - Employment via a study association (project form).
  - Fee per assignment (for those already working, e.g., retired nurses).

**Evaluation**:

  - Number of contacts per guide and month (target: 50–100).
  - Type of cases (lifestyle, mental health, guiding, social).
  - Change in the number of primary care center visits in the pilot area compared to a control area.
  - User satisfaction (survey).
  - Cost per contact compared to a doctor's visit or a nurse's visit.

## Budget and financing

| Item | Amount (per municipality, 3 years) | Total 3 municipalities |
|------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Training of health guides (10 participants × SEK 15,000) | SEK 150,000 | SEK 450,000 |
| Salary costs (4 guides × 0.5 FTE × SEK 350,000/year) | SEK 700,000/year | SEK 6.3 million (over 3 years) |
| Supervision and continuing education | SEK 50,000/year | SEK 450,000 |
| Materials and marketing | SEK 30,000/year | SEK 270,000 |
| Evaluation (research support) | SEK 100,000 (one-time) | SEK 300,000 |
| **Total per municipality (3 years)** | **approx. SEK 2.8 million** | **approx. SEK 8.4 million** |

**Financing**:

  - **Municipal public health funds**: Many municipalities already have a budget for health-promoting work.
  - **Regional grants**: The regions can finance the pilot as part of the transition to "Nära vård" (Close care).
  - **EU funds**: The ESF (European Social Fund) gladly finances projects that create new professional roles within social innovation.
  - **Savings**: Every avoided doctor's visit saves \~SEK 2,000. If each guide prevents 200 doctor's visits per year, the investment pays for itself.

**Note**: This is a **strategic investment** that in the long term reduces costs for both the municipality and the region.

## Regulatory changes required

1.  **Professional title**: Apply to the National Board of Health and Welfare (Socialstyrelsen) to make **Health guide** a protected professional title (like assistant nurse). This requires a national educational standard and quality assurance.

2.  **The Popular Education Ordinance (Folkbildningsförordningen)**: Task study associations and folk high schools with developing national syllabuses for health guides.

3.  **The Health and Medical Services Act (HSL)**: Clarify that health guides are not covered by HSL (they do not perform healthcare), but that they can operate in close collaboration with the healthcare system.

4.  **Secrecy and confidentiality**: Health guides are not covered by the strict secrecy of healthcare, but should be bound by confidentiality by contract. A simple template for consent and information management is produced.

5.  **Labor law and collective agreements**: Negotiate an appropriate salary level and form of employment with the trade unions (e.g., Vision, Kommunal).

## Expected resistance and answers

### "This is a de-skilling of healthcare – we need more nurses, not more laypeople."

**Answer**: Health guides do not replace nurses or doctors. They **relieve** them so that the professionals can devote themselves to what they are trained for. A nurse should not spend 20 minutes explaining the "plate model" (dietary guidelines) to a healthy person – a health guide can do that.

### "Who takes responsibility if the guide gives incorrect advice?"

**Answer**: The training clearly emphasizes the boundary against medical advice. The guide always refers to 1177 or a primary care center in case of symptoms. The work is carried out under the supervision of licensed personnel (e.g., a nurse who is available for consultation).

### "There are already health coaches – why should taxpayers finance this?"

**Answer**: Private health coaches primarily reach well-resourced groups. Health guides in the public sector reach the entire population and reduce health inequalities. Furthermore, it is economically profitable for society: prevention is cheaper than treatment.

### "The municipalities cannot afford more employees."

**Answer**: The investment is small compared to the cost of ill health. A single sick leave costs the municipality more than a half-time position for a health guide for an entire year. In addition, the regions can co-finance because they save money on reduced healthcare consumption.

### "Are there international examples?"

**Answer**: Yes. **Community Health Workers** in the US and **Social Prescribing Link Workers** in the UK are established professional roles with clear evidence of reduced healthcare consumption and improved public health.

## Swedish examples to build upon

  - **Health coaches in Region Skåne**: Briefly trained coaches working with lifestyle habits at primary care centers.
  - **"Fixer" services (Fixartjänster) in many municipalities**: Craftspeople who help the elderly with practical things – a role model for a practical, low-threshold role.
  - **The libraries' investment in "Health squares" (Hälsotorg)**: Librarians who guide people to health information – a natural collaborative partner.
  - **Public health guides in Gothenburg**: Pilot project where long-term unemployed people were trained as health guides in vulnerable areas.

## What you can do tomorrow

**If you are a municipal politician**: Submit a motion to introduce a trial with health guides in your municipality. Use existing funds for public health or labor market measures.

**If you work within popular education**: Develop a syllabus for health guides and apply for funding from the Swedish National Council of Adult Education (Folkbildningsrådet). Invite the municipality as a partner.

**If you work at a primary care center**: Think about which patient groups frequently return with non-medical needs. Suggest to your manager that you try having a health guide on site one day a week.
