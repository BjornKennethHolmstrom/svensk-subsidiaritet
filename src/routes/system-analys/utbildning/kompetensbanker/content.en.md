# Competence banks

[← Back to the main article](..)

**Summary**: A digital platform (open source) where citizens can offer teaching based on demonstrated expertise rather than formal qualifications. A retired engineer can hold a workshop in physics, a florist can teach flower arrangement, a newly arrived immigrant with agricultural experience can show how to farm in the local climate. Verification is done through peer reviews, reputation, and a simple background check (criminal record extract). Municipalities, schools, associations, and individuals can book the competence bearers for a small fee. The goal: to break the certification monopoly and provide the local, practical knowledge that the standardized school misses – at a low cost.

## The mechanism

Today, it is basically impossible for a person without a teaching license to teach in school, even if the person possesses unique expertise. A skilled goldsmith, an experienced structural engineer, a knowledgeable mushroom expert – everyone has knowledge that students would greatly benefit from, but formal requirements prevent them from sharing it. At the same time, the school struggles with motivation, relevance, and its connection to working life.

**The measure**: A **competence bank** is a digital platform where:

  - **Competence bearers** (citizens over 18) create a profile with their skills and experiences. No formal grades are required – they can show a portfolio, work samples, or a video presentation.
  - **Verification** is done through:
      - *Peer review*: previous participants rate the competence bearer (1–5 stars) after a completed activity.
      - *Background check*: an extract from the criminal record is required to work with children (just like for sports coaches, field assistants, etc.).
      - *Test period*: new competence bearers can hold a first workshop for free or at a reduced price to build a reputation.
  - **Clients** (municipalities, schools, youth centers, libraries, associations, parent groups) can search among profiles and book competence bearers for single occasions (2–4 hours) or longer courses (6–8 sessions).
  - **Compensation**: The competence bearer receives a fee (e.g., SEK 300–500/hour). The platform takes a small commission (10%) to cover operation and marketing.
  - **Connection to the school**: Teachers can book competence bearers as part of the teaching – e.g., a florist who teaches biology through flowers, a carpenter who teaches mathematics through angles and measurements. The activities are documented in the student's portfolio.

**What the competence bank solves**:

| Problem today | The competence bank's solution |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| The certification monopoly prevents expertise from reaching the school | Teaching without a license is permitted as a guest intervention |
| The school lacks connection to local working life | Competence bearers come from real professions |
| Practical knowledge is valued lowly in the curriculum | Students encounter crafts, farming, and technology in reality |
| Newly arrived immigrants with cutting-edge expertise cannot use it | The competence bank creates a path into working life (extra income, network) |
| Under-resourced areas have fewer study visits and guests | A digital platform makes competence available to all municipalities |

**Examples of competence bearers**:

| Competence | Background | Possible lesson/workshop |
|-----------|----------|------------------------|
| Mathematics in the construction industry | Retired structural engineer | Calculate timber consumption, angles, volumes – on a real construction site |
| Mycology (mushrooms) | Former mushroom inspector | Species identification, ecology, cooking with mushrooms |
| Textile crafts | Syrian immigrant woman with traditional embroidery art | Sew your own painting, learn different stitches, cultural background |
| Programming | Self-taught game developer | Build a simple game in Scratch, guest lecture about the career path |
| Gardening | Biodynamic grower | Sowing, soil, crop rotation – in the school's allotments |

## Pilot design – 5 regions first

**Pilot phase 1 (years 1‑3)**: Five regions or larger municipalities develop a local competence bank (or share a common platform). The pilots are chosen to represent different parts of Sweden (city, rural area, north, south, high proportion of newly arrived immigrants).

**Possible pilot locations**:

1.  **City of Stockholm** – big city mix, many skilled professionals, test scaling
2.  **Jämtland (region)** – sparsely populated area, need to activate the competence of the elderly and newly arrived immigrants
3.  **Botkyrka municipality** – high proportion of foreign-born, many with unutilized work experience
4.  **Region Västra Götaland** (Rural Network) – focus on crafts and small business owners
5.  **Norrbotten** – mining and industrial competence, Sami knowledge

**Organizational model**:

  - The region or municipality finances the development of a simple platform (open source, can be reused by others).
  - A **competence broker** (0.5–1 FTE) is responsible for recruiting and quality-assuring competence bearers, as well as marketing the platform to schools and associations.
  - The platform uses existing digital infrastructure: BankID for login, Swish for payments, peer review systems similar to Uber or Airbnb.
  - Compensation to competence bearers: SEK 400/hour (average). The platform takes SEK 40/hour (10%) for operation and marketing.

**Quality assurance**:

  - The competence bearer must show an extract from the criminal record (to work with children).
  - The school or client rates the competence bearer after a completed assignment (1–5 stars, comments). Low ratings lead to suspension.
  - The first assignment can be free of charge for the school (subsidized by the pilot budget) to test and build a reputation.
  - Insurance: competence bearers are covered by the municipality's/region's liability insurance during the assignment (regulated in contract).

**Evaluation**:

  - Number of active competence bearers and number of bookings per month.
  - User satisfaction (schools, teachers, competence bearers) measured in ratings (target: \>4.5 out of 5).
  - Change in students' motivation (teacher survey) linked to subjects that received visits from the competence bank.
  - The proportion of competence bearers with a foreign background or without formal higher education (target: \>50% to show that networks outside traditional ones have been reached).
  - Cost per lesson hour compared to a regular teacher's hourly cost (approx. SEK 800–1,000). Target: under SEK 500 including platform fee.

|------|-------------------------------|-------------------|

| Marketing (information campaigns to schools, associations) | SEK 200,000/year | SEK 1,000,000/year |
| Subsidy of first assignments (500 bookings at SEK 400) | SEK 200,000/year | SEK 1,000,000/year |
| Evaluation | SEK 200,000/year | SEK 1,000,000/year |

**Financing**: SEK 9 million in the first year is a very small investment compared to the school budget (SEK 300 billion) and can be taken from regional development funds (EU funds, the Swedish Agency for Economic and Regional Growth) or state grants for skills supply. If the competence bank in each region facilitates 5,000 hours of teaching per year (at SEK 400/hour), that is SEK 2 million in fees to competence bearers – a direct contribution to the local economy.

## Regulatory changes required

1.  **Clarification in the Education Act (2010:800)** – no prohibition against bringing in external guest teachers without a license, but a clarification can help: *"The school may engage external knowledge bearers to supplement regular teaching. These do not need to have a teaching license, but must be able to show an extract from the criminal record if they work with children."* (This is already possible today, but many principals are unsure.)
2.  **Secrecy and GDPR** – the platform must comply with the General Data Protection Regulation. Simple user policy (consent from competence bearers and clients). No sensitive personal data is stored.
3.  **Ordinance on state grants** – The Swedish National Agency for Education or the Swedish Agency for Economic and Regional Growth is tasked with allocating funds for the start-up of competence banks in regions (according to the pilot model).
4.  **The Swedish Tax Agency** – fees to competence bearers (under SEK 10,000 per year) can be paid out without tax deduction if it is a minor side income (similar rules as for sports coaches). Simplify to reduce administration.

## Expected resistance and answers

### "This undermines the teaching profession – anyone can call themselves a competence bearer."

**Answer**: The competence bank does not replace the teacher. The teacher remains in the classroom, plans the lesson, and integrates the competence bearer's element into regular teaching. The competence bearer is a guest, just like an author who visits the school or an engineer who gives a lecture. The difference is that the competence bank makes it easy for teachers to find and book relevant guests.

### "The union will protest – risk of dumping teachers' salaries."

**Answer**: The competence bearer's fee (SEK 400/hour) is lower than a teacher's hourly cost (SEK 800–1,000), but the competence bearer has no responsibility for planning, grading, follow-up, or classroom management. It is a specialized supplementary effort, not a replacement. On the contrary, several teachers express frustration that they have to be experts on everything – the competence bank relieves them.

### "The quality could be bad – no guarantee that competence bearers can teach."

**Answer**: Peer reviews and background checks are simpler forms of quality assurance. The school can also request that the competence bearer hold a trial lesson (subsidized). In the worst case, a lesson is less successful – it is a small risk compared to the potential. And the system is self-regulating: competence bearers with low ratings will not get new assignments.

### "Municipalities and schools will not use the platform – it's easier to book study visits as usual."

**Answer**: Today's system for booking guest lecturers is ad hoc – you call someone you know or google. The competence bank offers a uniform interface, a rating system, and easy invoicing. For schools that lack local networks (e.g., in vulnerable areas), it is a lifeline. The pilot will have to show if the demand exists.

### "Are there Swedish examples?"

**Answer**: Yes, several similar initiatives at the experimental stage. **"The City Library's makerspace"** in Malmö books local craftspeople for workshops. **"Building technology in school"** (The Swedish Construction Federation) mediates construction workers to schools, but is not a digital platform. **"Skillshift"** (private actor) matches professionals with schools, but is small. The closest internationally is **"Teach for America"** (but that is teacher training) and **"Outschool"** (USA, digital courses). The competence bank is a Swedish, open, and decentralized model.

## Swedish examples to build upon

  - **Sports coaches in associations** – every weekend, thousands of volunteer coaches lead children and youth in football, handball, etc., without a teaching license. Works excellently. The same principle can be applied to academic subjects.
  - **Pensioner organizations** (SPF, PRO) – many pensioners have unique professional experience and time to share. The competence bank can collaborate with them.
  - **Study associations** (ABF, NBV, Sensus) – have long experience in organizing study circles with laypeople as leaders. The model exists.
  - **Norway's "Kompetansebanken"** (Vox, now Skills Norway) – similar concept for adult education, but not integrated with the school. Sweden can be inspired.

## What you can do tomorrow

**If you are a principal or teacher**: Take an inventory – what local professionals, pensioners, craftspeople, or newly arrived immigrants could contribute to teaching? Call one up and ask: "Can you come and talk about your job?" No need for a digital platform – start analog.

**If you are a regional or municipal politician**: Allocate a small sum (SEK 200,000) to hire a competence broker on trial. Ask them to map unutilized competence in the region and match it with schools. Evaluate after one year.

**If you are a pensioner or professional**: Join a study association or contact your local school directly. Tell them what you can teach. Offer to hold a workshop. You don't need a platform – just the willingness to share.
